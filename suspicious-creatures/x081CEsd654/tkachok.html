Mykola Tkachuk: Biography

Mykola Tkachuk, born in a Ukrainian village where fresh hay smelled at every step and there was an unstoppable flow of thoughts about eternal questions, was a man of an incredibly complex and sometimes even tragicomic fate. He lived with the idea that the best life was when you developed a game, wrote about politics and pretty girls, and of course helped your mother with the garden, while all the hustle and bustle of the world continued to spin, oblivious to his struggle with his own reality. Mykola always dreamed of creating a huge computer game that would change the industry. He even studied programming at university, but the problem was that he constantly put everything off until tomorrow. Postponed the development of the game because I had to go to the store and buy new jeans at a discount, because the old ones were already so worn that they looked like fragments from some ancient archaeological expedition. I postponed the development of the game because I had to help my mother in the village with tomatoes, and this is more important. I postponed the development of the game, because a new free game was just released, and Mykola definitely had to collect it for his collection.

Unraveling the Mystery of Mykola Tkachuk

Mykola's life is a series of unexpected turns. The delay in developing his life-changing game became a metaphor for his existence. A man caught between procrastination and destiny, Mykola’s true legacy remains locked in the labyrinth of his own choices. Perhaps one day, the world will finally unlock the code that is Mykola.

Unraveling the Mystery

And now, once again sitting at the computer with a mountain of plastic coffee cups and empty packets of chips, Mykola opens a new game. Now this was a game about politics and pretty girls, where every choice you made affected what your career would be and what relationships you would eventually form with the characters. He conducted an interesting dialogue about democratic values ​​with a fictional political figure, completely oblivious to the fact that the clock was already ticking to 2 am. The most interesting thing is that while thinking, Mykola came to the philosophical idea that politics and pretty girls are actually very similar concepts: they both change so quickly that you just don't have time to understand what is happening. And so, after such deep thinking, he decided that writing texts about these two topics is not only his vocation, but also a way to express his protest power. Mykola once tried to return to physical form. He decided to go to the horizontal bar and do at least one push-up on the horizontal bar. But here the real comedy began: the first attempt - the hands could not stand again, and Mykola, entangled in his own body, remained hanging on the horizontal bar like a marionette. That is how he remained weak physically, but with unlimited power in the world of ideas and reflections.

HisTory

The real problem was that all these ideas, texts and development of the game kept getting postponed. He wanted to create a computer game about his village, but there were constantly new situations, new games, new discounts on discounts in stores, new political scandals, new problems in the garden. And it seems that the development of the game was so far from his hands that it is no longer clear what will come first: his new game or the promised "tomorrow" again?

And, despite all this, Mykola was a philosopher. He lived like that philosopher who knows everything but does nothing. His games, texts, music and politics turned into an endless cycle of delays and postponements. However, in the depths of his soul, Mykola knew that one day he would create everything. Just not today.
